## Disqualifying yield sources (we avoid all four)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="❌ Ponzi-like yield-bearing stables (circular yield dependencies)">
    **Not used.** SaveToWin's 70% fixed sleeve is PT-ONyc — a principal token redeemable at par at maturity, backed by real-world reinsurance premiums. No YBS wrapping, no circular collateralisation, no dependency on another YBS that depends on us. The 30% floating sleeve is Kamino USDC Main, a senior over-collateralised lending reserve.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="❌ Junior tranche / insurance pool designs (RLP, jrUSDe)">
    **Not used.** Neither sleeve buys into a first-loss junior tranche. PT-ONyc holders are senior claimants on the reinsurance premium stream — not equity-tranche underwriters. Kamino USDC Main depositors are senior lenders, not subordinate risk-takers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="❌ DEX LP vaults (JLP, HLP, LLP)">
    **Not used.** No DEX liquidity provision, no impermanent-loss exposure. Capital sits in PT bonds and senior lending reserves, both of which have a defined principal redemption path independent of market-maker inventory risk.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="❌ High-leverage looping (health rate below 1.05 on non-hardcoded oracle assets)">
    **Not used.** Kamino deposits are supply-only — no borrowing, no looping, no leverage. Health rate is effectively infinite. Exponent PT holdings are spot positions, not margined.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
